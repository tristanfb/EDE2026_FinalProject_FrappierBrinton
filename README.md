# EDE2026_FinalProject_FrappierBrinton
Repository for the final project for Environmental Data Exploration class Spring 2026 by Tristan Frappier-Brinton. Project assessing factors influencing burned area and fire spread in Ankarafantsika National Park, Madagascar

The associated project uses logistic regression of classified burn images derived from LANDSAT imagery to estimate the effect of the conservation actions of a local NGO (Planet Madagascar) on burn probability. 

Other datasets: Elevation was obtained from NASA's ASTER DEM. Slope and aspect were derived from the ASTER DEM using the respective functions in ArcGIS Pro during previous work. Slope was calculated at 90 m resolution, as we believed this scale to be less susceptible to pixel-level error in elevation and more relevant to fire behaviour.  Road lines shapefiles were obtained directly from local residents and researchers in ANP. Land Cover classifications were obtained from the European Space Agency's WorldCover website (https://esa-worldcover.org/en/data-access) for the year 2020 (before any 2021 fires occurred). ESA data were downloaded at 10 m resolution and resampled to 30 m. I also obtained shapefiles for ANP (obtained from the Protected Planet database of protected areas) and the active management area of Planet Madagascar (obtained directly from Planet MAdagascar). All publically-available datasets used are open access and free to use.
